Level 1: Starter
Your first sales -- get in the door
$150-$500

One small, specific deliverable. Low risk for the client. Designed to prove you can deliver.

  • → Single landing page or website section
  • → 5-10 social media graphics
  • → One email sequence (3-5 emails)
  • → Logo or brand refresh
  • → Video editing for 3-5 short clips
Why this works
It's easy for the client to say yes. Low price = low risk. You get a portfolio piece AND a potential upsell.
Level 2: Package
Bundled deliverables with clear scope
$500-$1,500

Multiple deliverables bundled together. Solves a bigger problem. Higher value, higher price.

  • → Full website (3-5 pages)
  • → Brand kit (logo + colors + fonts + guidelines)
  • → Content package (20 posts + captions + hashtags)
  • → Full funnel setup (landing page + emails + automation)
  • → Video production package (10 edited clips + thumbnails)
The upgrade path
"You loved the landing page? Let me build the full website + set up the email sequence to match. It's $1,200 for the complete package."
Level 3: Retainer
Monthly recurring revenue
$500-$2,000/mo

Ongoing monthly work. Predictable income for you. Consistent support for them. The holy grail of freelancing.

  • → Social media management (content + posting + engagement)
  • → Monthly content creation (X posts per month)
  • → Ongoing website maintenance + updates
  • → Email marketing management
  • → Video editing on retainer (X videos per month)
The pitch
"Instead of hiring and managing someone full-time, I handle your entire social media for $1,500/month. You save time, money, and headaches."

How to Say the Price

The Wrong Way
"So... I was thinking maybe like $500? But it depends on the scope and we can always adjust if that's too much. I'm flexible on pricing. Let me know what works for you?"
Uncertain Apologetic Weak
The Right Way
"Based on everything we discussed, the investment is $500. That includes the landing page, 2 rounds of revisions, and delivery in 7 days. I can send the invoice today and start this week."
Confident Specific Clear next step

State the price. Shut up. Let them respond.

Common Objections + Responses

"That's more than I expected"
"I understand. Let me break down what you're getting: [re-state deliverables]. Based on the results, this typically pays for itself within [timeframe]."
"I can find someone cheaper on Fiverr"
"You definitely can. The difference is I'm building this specifically for YOUR audience, with a strategy behind it -- not just a template. You get a partner, not a vendor."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Absolutely. We can split it into 2 payments -- 50% to start, 50% on delivery. That work for you?"
"Let me think about it"
"Of course. What specifically would help you decide? I'm happy to answer any questions."
