You learned this in Module 8 -- the 5-Step Conversation. OPEN, DISCOVER, BRIDGE, OFFER, HANDLE.

The Qualify Step

Ask these 4 questions before committing to a call:

Q1
"What's the main thing you need help with?"
Q2
"Have you worked with someone on this before?"
Q3
"What's your timeline for getting this done?"
Q4
"Do you have a budget range in mind?"

If they can't answer these, they're not ready. Don't waste your time.

Steps 4-5: Propose & Price

After the call, send a simple proposal.

Not a 20-page document. Not a contract. Just a clear summary of:

  • What you'll do
  • What they'll get
  • When they'll get it
  • What it costs

Send it within 24 hours of the call. Speed = professionalism.

Template

GOAL
"Help Coach Danny get more online clients through a professional landing page and content system."
DELIVERABLES

4. Google Business optimization
TIMELINE
2 weeks from project start
INVESTMENT
RM 2,500 (50% upfront, 50% on delivery)

Steps 6-7: Close & Onboard

CLOSE

  • Follow up 24-48 hours after sending proposal
  • "Did you get a chance to look at the proposal?"
  • Answer any remaining questions
  • Get a clear yes or no. Don't chase ghosts.

ONBOARD

  • Collect payment (50% upfront)
  • Set clear expectations and timeline
  • Gather all assets (logos, photos, copy)
  • Start delivering. Communicate progress.

How to Set Your First Price

The "Would I Be Embarrassed?" Test:

  • Too low? "Would I be embarrassed telling a friend how little I charged?"
  • Too high? "Would I be embarrassed if the client saw how long this actually took me?"
  • Just right? "Am I proud of the work AND the price?"

Start at a price that feels slightly uncomfortable. That's usually the right one.

How to Say the Price

WRONG WAY
"So, um... I was thinking maybe... would something around, like... RM 500 be okay? I can go lower if that's too much..."

Uncertain. Apologetic. Signals low value.

RIGHT WAY
"For everything we discussed -- the landing page, 4 posts, and Google setup -- the investment is RM 1,500. I take 50% upfront and 50% on delivery. Want me to send the proposal over?"

Confident. Clear. Immediate next step.

Common Objections + Responses

"That's too expensive."
"I understand. Which part of the package would you like to start with? We can begin smaller and scale up."
"I can get it cheaper on Fiverr."
"You can. The difference is I understand your business, I'll customize everything, and I'm accountable to your results -- not just a deliverable."
"Let me think about it."
"Of course. I'll send the proposal and follow up on [specific day]. Is there anything specific holding you back?"
"I don't have the budget right now."
"No problem. Would you be open to a smaller starter project? I can do just the landing page for RM 500 and we go from there."

Salary Negotiation

The same principles apply:

  • Know your number before the interview
  • Let them go first -- "What's the range for this role?"
  • State it with confidence -- "Based on my skills and what this role requires, I'm looking at RM [X]"
  • Back it up -- show your portfolio, your 30/60/90 plan, your AI skills

Your portfolio is your negotiation leverage.
