Day 3 Framework

The 5-Step Conversation

How to turn a DM reply into a paying client. No sleazy tactics. Just a real conversation with structure.

The Structure
1
OPEN
Build rapport, be human
2
DISCOVER
Ask questions, listen deeply
3
BRIDGE
Connect their problem to your solution
4
OFFER
Present something specific to them
5
HANDLE
Address concerns honestly
1
OPEN
Build rapport. Be human.

The first 30 seconds set the tone. Don't jump into business. Be a person first.

  • -- Find something in common
  • -- Reference their content or background
  • -- Ask a genuine question about them
Example
"Hey Sarah! I saw your post about transitioning from corporate to freelance -- I did the same thing 2 years ago. How's the journey going?"
2
DISCOVER
Ask questions. Listen.

Your goal is to understand their situation, pain, and goals. Not to talk about yourself.

"Whoever talks most, LOSES."
The best salespeople listen 80% and talk 20%. Your job is to ask the right questions and let them tell you exactly what they need.

The Discovery Questions

Ask these. Then shut up and listen.

  • 1 "What are you working on right now?" -- understand their context
  • 2 "What's the biggest challenge with that?" -- find the pain
  • 3 "How long has this been a problem?" -- understand urgency
  • 4 "What have you tried so far?" -- learn what failed
  • 5 "What would the ideal outcome look like?" -- define success
  • 6 "What happens if you don't fix this?" -- build urgency
  • 7 "Are you ready to invest in solving this?" -- qualify budget
3
BRIDGE
"Based on what you told me..."

Now connect their problem to your solution. Use their words, not yours.

The bridge sentence uses what they told you to transition naturally into your offer.

Bridge Template
"Based on what you told me -- you're getting views but not converting followers into clients -- I think the issue is your landing page. I've helped 3 coaches fix exactly this."

The bridge makes the offer feel natural, not pushy.

4
OFFER
Specific to their problem

Don't offer "everything you do." Offer one specific thing that solves the problem they just told you about.

  • X "I do social media, websites, branding, and content..."
  • ✓ "I can rebuild your landing page to convert visitors into bookings. Takes about a week."
The Rule
One problem. One solution. One clear next step.
5
HANDLE
Address concerns honestly

Objections are not rejections -- they're requests for more information.

"It's too expensive" → "I understand. What would it cost you to NOT fix this for another 3 months?"
"I need to think about it" → "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? Maybe I can help."
"I've been burned before" → "That's fair. Here's what makes this different: [specific guarantee or proof]."

Think Like a Doctor

The Doctor Analogy
Greeting = OPEN (build rapport)
Diagnosis = DISCOVER (ask questions)
Explanation = BRIDGE (connect problem to solution)
Prescription = OFFER (specific treatment)
Reassurance = HANDLE (address fears)

A doctor doesn't prescribe before diagnosing.
Neither should you.
