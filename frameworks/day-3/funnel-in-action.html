1
Contact

The first touch. They either find you (content, referral) or you find them (outreach).

Key metric
How many new conversations did you START this week?
2
Qualify

Not everyone is a good fit. Filter fast. Don't waste time on people who can't or won't pay.

Key question
Do they have the problem, budget, and urgency?
3
Call

Use the 5-Step Conversation: Open, Discover, Bridge, Offer, Handle.

Can be a video call, voice call, or even a structured DM conversation.

Goal
Understand their problem deeply enough to make a specific offer
4
Propose

Keep it simple. One page. Bullet points. Not a 20-page document.

The proposal confirms what you discussed -- it's not new information.

Rule
If the proposal surprises them, you didn't do Step 3 right
5
Price

State the price confidently. Tie it to the value, not the hours.

  • X "It'll be around $500... but we can negotiate"
  • ✓ "The investment is $500. Here's what you get."
6
Close

Ask for the decision. Don't wait for them to volunteer.

Closing Line
"Does this work for you? If so, I can send the invoice and we can start this week."
7
Onboard

The first 48 hours define the entire relationship. Start strong.

  • ✓ Send welcome message + recap of what was agreed
  • ✓ Collect everything you need (access, assets, info)
  • ✓ Set clear timeline and expectations
  • ✓ Deliver a quick win in the first 48 hours
  • ✓ Schedule the first check-in

A great onboarding = referrals + retention.

The Qualify Questions

Ask these before investing time in a call

Problem
"What's the main challenge you're facing?"
Do they have a problem you can solve?
Urgency
"How soon do you need this fixed?"
Are they ready to act NOW or "someday"?
Budget
"Do you have a budget in mind for this?"
Can they actually afford to pay you?
Authority
"Are you the decision maker on this?"
Are you talking to the right person?

If they fail 2+ of these, move on. Your time is valuable.

The Simple Proposal

One page. That's it.

Proposal Template
1. The Problem
"You told me [their problem in their words]..."
2. The Solution
"Here's what I'll do to fix it: [3-5 bullet points]"
3. The Timeline
"This will take [X days/weeks] to complete"
4. The Investment
"The total investment is [price]"
5. Next Step
"If this works for you, I'll send the invoice and we start [date]"

The Funnel in Action

1. Contact → 2. Qualify → 3. Call → 4. Propose → 5. Price → 6. Close → 7. Onboard

Every step has one job: get them to the next step.

Don't try to close on the first message. Don't propose before the call. Follow the funnel.
